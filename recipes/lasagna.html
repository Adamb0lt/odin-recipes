Lasagna recipe

HomePage

Descripton

Lasagna is a classic Italian casserole dish featuring alternating, baked layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, savory meat or vegetable ragù, creamy béchamel or cheese (ricotta/mozzarella), and Parmesan. Originating from ancient Roman cuisine, it is typically baked until golden and bubbly, offering a rich, comforting, and melt-in-your-mouth texture.

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Marinara sauce
  • Egg
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh basil or parsley

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  4. Add ground beef or sausage and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in marinara sauce and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with egg, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce in a baking dish.
  8. Add a layer of noodles, then ricotta mixture, then mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat layers until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and cheese on top.
  10. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  11. Remove foil and bake another 15–20 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  12. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
